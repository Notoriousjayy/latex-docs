\entry{Startup Name:}{Algorithmic Stock Trading System}
\entry{Working Business Idea:}{Build a disciplined algorithmic stock trading platform that unifies market data ingestion, research, backtesting, paper trading, live execution, and risk controls into one repeatable operating system.}

\begin{minipage}[t]{0.49\textwidth}
\entry{Problem:}{Independent systematic traders often rely on disconnected scripts, spreadsheets, broker dashboards, and notebooks, which makes strategy validation, execution control, and risk management inconsistent.}
\entry{Value Proposition:}{Provide a single workflow from data to signal to order to review so strategy development is more reproducible, execution is more controlled, and risk management is enforced by system design instead of manual discipline alone.}
\entry{Current Stage:}{Validation / MVP definition / founder-operated build}
\end{minipage}\hfill
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.49\textwidth}
\entry{Target Customer:}{Initial user is the founder-operator. Later customer potential includes advanced independent traders, small quantitative research teams, and boutique trading operations that need a disciplined execution and research platform.}
\entry{Revenue Model:}{Near term, value is generated through founder-operated trading performance and system capability. Longer term options include subscription software, premium analytics, hosted tooling, or research and execution support services.}
\entry{Current Focus:}{Define the first strategy wedge, build the research and backtesting foundation, stand up paper trading, and codify hard risk controls before deploying real capital.}
\end{minipage}

\textbf{Top 3 Immediate Priorities}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=0pt,label={}]
    \item \textbf{Priority 1.} Finish the minimum viable research and backtesting environment with reproducible data, signals, and performance reports.
    \item \textbf{Priority 2.} Define non-negotiable risk rules for exposure, position sizing, drawdown, and kill-switch conditions.
    \item \textbf{Priority 3.} Validate one or two strategies in paper trading with realistic assumptions for slippage, costs, and execution quality.
\end{itemize}
